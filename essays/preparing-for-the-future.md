Most courses I have taken as a computer science student taught me proofs, theories, and concepts, and how they apply in the real world. For instance, “this algorithm does X” or “this is how your computer interprets Y” but I often wondered how this knowledge is used the professional field. As a student, it was a bit overwhelming to study material that wasn’t being practiced as it would in the professional sense, thus I certainly had no idea which career options to consider. ICS 314 taught me the necessary software engineering concepts such as design, quality assurance, project management, etc., but ICS 414 gave me an opportunity to experience working on a professional project with real customers. I also got a taste of what the future of tech jobs may entail: remote work. After taking ICS 414, I am confident that software engineering is a suitable career for me. I learned that communication is the foremost vital component in collaborative work, the benefits of issue driven project management reviews, and the significance of ethics in software engineering. 

The past few semesters have certainly been atypical due to COVID-19, but there is one key benefit of taking a class akin to ICS 414 during a time like this, and it’s that we have firsthand experience of the future of software engineering jobs. Remote work for tech jobs is quickly becoming more permanent and it comes with many benefits such as less physical office space and resources required to run a company, lowered transportation-related greenhouse gas emissions, and the freedoms and advantages of location independence. On the other hand, remote work also places a greater stress on the importance of communication among team members. We need to be on top of all forms of communication including email, Zoom, text, etc., and be sure that we communicate and resolve issues effectively.

In my ICS 414 course, I experienced an incident where communication was not on par across my team members and myself. The issue was that two team members worked on the same GitHub branch without realizing the other member was also doing work in that branch, thus other issues arose as a result. Not only did this impact our efficiency and goals for the project but it also put a strain on the relationship among group members. We realized that the problem lied in communication and we addressed the problem to prevent similar incidents from happening in the future. We scheduled more weekly meetings, created a shared document for daily check-ins to document progress of issues and goals for each member, and came to an agreement that if any future problems or concerns of a similar manner were to occur, we would communicate our problems to each other as soon as it arises. Though this incident was unfortunate and negatively impacted the quality of our project, I am thankful for the learning experience. 

Frequent issue driven project management reviews were valuable in the process of our project. It allowed us the opportunity to review team members’ code to greater understand particular functions of the project, and to clearly identify issues that need work and what goals should be set for the next milestone in the project. As these reviews are worked on independently then shared with one another at the same time, concerns regarding biased and influenced reviews are eliminated. This produces honest and beneficial feedback for the project thus openly pinpointing issues that need to be corrected. These reviews were also a great way to assess progress and resolve problems before milestones deadlines. 

The most valuable insight I gained personally from working with HEI directly is the significance of ethic responsibility in the technology/software engineering field. This project allowed me to recognize the kinds of impacts we can make on the community and the world. It was always a goal of mine to use my knowledge and degree to create a more sustainable and conscious community, and this project with HEI is exactly the type of work I’d gladly to devote my time to. Following this project, I’ve reflected on further ways that applications and software can create a more sustainable environment. I hope to achieve this is as a future software engineer.
